# X/Twitter threads: Why I built SoberSteps

Founder angle. No hype. 5–7 tweets each.

---

## Thread 1: The gap

1/ I built SoberSteps because every app I tried was either a guilt trip or a cheerleader.

Neither helped at 3 AM.

2/ Streaks are fine. But when you break one, you don't need "Day 1 again" slapped on your face. You need something that doesn't add to the pile.

3/ I wanted an app that said: "You're here. Ciekawe, co przyniesie ten dzień."

Not: "You failed. Start over."

4/ So I built it. Craving surf, 3 AM SOS, journal, letters to yourself. Nothing fancy. Just: stay with yourself when it's hardest.

5/ It's free for the core stuff. If you ever need it, it's there.

https://soberstepsdev.github.io/sobersteps/

---

## Thread 2: Curiosity over willpower

1/ Most recovery apps sell willpower. "Stay strong. Fight the urge. You can do this."

I don't believe that model anymore.

2/ I built SoberSteps around something simpler: curiosity.

"What happens if I just stay a little longer?"

3/ Not goals. Not targets. Just: ciekawe, co się wydarzy.

That shift changed how I think about the whole road.

4/ The app has streaks and milestones. But they're markers, not judges. 80% wystarczy — 80% is enough.

5/ No promises. No "quit in 30 days." Just tools for when you want to come back to yourself.

6/ If that resonates, take a look.

https://soberstepsdev.github.io/sobersteps/

---

## Thread 3: Return to yourself

1/ SoberSteps started as a personal project. I needed something that wasn't on the market.

2/ Existing apps were either clinical (triggering) or motivational (exhausting). I wanted neither.

3/ I wanted: quiet. A place that didn't shame or hype. That said "you're already here" instead of "you should be further."

4/ So I built: craving surf, 3 AM screen, journal, letters to future-you, a 30-day path. Simple building blocks.

5/ The philosophy: Uśmiech ↔ Perspektywa ↔ Droga. Curiosity, perspective, road. No toxic positivity. No moralizing.

6/ It's not for everyone. But if you're tired of being told to fight harder, it might be.

https://soberstepsdev.github.io/sobersteps/
